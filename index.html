🌍 Survival Guide: Supermarket & Restaurant Tips

Survival guide by Nicolás García-Sampedro Docampo

🛒 At the Supermarket

Vocabulary

  • Fruits: apples (manzanas), bananas (plátanos), oranges (naranjas)
  • Vegetables: carrots (zanahorias), tomatoes (tomates), onions (cebollas)
  • Dairy: milk (leche), cheese (queso), yogurt (yogur)
  • Meat: chicken (pollo), beef (res), pork (cerdo)
  • Snacks: chips (papas fritas), cookies (galletas), chocolate (chocolate)
  • Beverages: water (agua), juice (jugo), coffee (café)
  • Numbers (for prices): uno (1), dos (2), tres (3), cuatro (4), cinco (5), seis (6), siete (7), ocho (8), nueve (9), diez (10), once (11), doce (12), trece (13), catorce (14), quince (15), dieciséis (16), diecisiete (17), dieciocho (18), diecinueve (19), veinte (20) ...

Useful Phrases

  • “Where can I find…?” → ¿Dónde puedo encontrar…?
  • “How much does this cost?” → ¿Cuánto cuesta esto?
  • “Do you accept card payments?” → ¿Aceptan pagos con tarjeta?
  • “Is this organic?” → ¿Esto es orgánico?
  • “Does this expire soon?” → ¿Este producto está cerca de caducar?
  • “Where is the checkout?” → ¿Dónde está la caja?
  • “Is there a discount for students?” → ¿Hay descuento para estudiantes?

Pro Tips

  • Bring a reusable bag – they’re often required!
  • Check for “2-for-1” deals (¡Oferta especial!).
  • Ask staff for help – say “Perdón, ¿me ayuda?”
  • Look for loyalty cards (tarjetas de cliente frecuente) for discounts.
  • Buy in bulk for cheaper prices on rice, pasta, or beans.
  • Supermarkets often close early on Sundays – plan ahead!

🍽️ At the Restaurant

Vocabulary

  • Appetizers: soup (sopa), salad (ensalada), bread (pan)
  • Main Course: pasta (pasta), grilled fish (pescado a la parrilla), stew (guiso)
  • Drinks: water (agua), juice (zumo), soda (gaseosa)
  • Desserts: cake (pastel), ice cream (helado), fruit (fruta)
  • Sides: fries (papas fritas), rice (arroz), vegetables (verduras)
  • Common Dishes: croquetas, paella, empanadas
  • Allergies: “I’m allergic to…” → Soy alérgico/a a…

Useful Phrases

  • “I’d like to order…” → Me gustaría pedir…
  • “Can I have the bill, please?” → ¿La cuenta, por favor?
  • “This is delicious!” → ¡Está delicioso!
  • “Is there a vegetarian option?” → ¿Hay una opción vegetariana?
  • “Could I have more bread, please?” → ¿Puedo tener más pan, por favor?
  • “I’m not hungry – just a small portion.” → No tengo hambre – solo una porción pequeña.
  • “What do you recommend?” → ¿Qué recomienda?

Pro Tips

  • Tipping: 10-15% is standard unless included.
  • Splitting the bill: Ask “¿Puedo dividir la cuenta?”
  • Wait times: “¿Tiene que esperar?” means “Do we need to wait?”
  • Some restaurants have “menú del día” (daily special) for cheaper meals.
  • Tap water is usually safe here – ask “¿Puedo tomar agua del grifo?”
  • Don’t rush! Meals are often relaxed and social.

📌 Cultural Notes

Polite Phrases

  • “Please” → Por favor
  • “Thank you” → Gracias
  • “Excuse me” → Disculpe (to get attention)
  • “You’re welcome” → De nada
  • “Sorry” → Lo siento
  • “I don’t speak Spanish well, sorry!” → ¡No hablo bien español, lo siento!

Local Customs

  • Supermarkets close early on Sundays here!
  • Restaurants may serve dinner later than you’re used to (e.g., 8–10 PM).
  • Double-check prices – taxes might not be included.
  • Locals greet with a kiss on the cheek (even strangers!). Say “¡Hola!” with a smile.
  • “Buen provecho!” is a common phrase before eating (like “Enjoy your meal!”).
  • Cash is still widely used – don’t rely only on cards.

Remember: Mistakes happen! Locals appreciate your effort to speak their language.

Buena suerte y disfruta tu comida! 😊
